How to Play

Step 1: Setup

Arrange objects (coins, matches, etc.) into different piles. Typically, start with 3-5 piles of varying sizes.

Step 2: Taking Turns

Players take turns removing any number of objects from a single pile of their choice.

Step 3: Strategy

Plan your moves carefully! Think about leaving specific pile configurations to force your opponent into unfavorable positions.

Step 4: Winning Move

The player who takes the last object wins or loses (depending on the variant being played).

Step 5: Practice

Master the game by learning common patterns and practicing different strategies against various opponents.

History of Nnim

Nnim is believed to be one of the oldest games in history, with origins dating back to ancient China. The name "Nim" was coined by Charles L. Bouton of Harvard University, who also developed the complete mathematical theory of the game in 1901.

The word "Nnim" is thought to come from an old Germanic word meaning "take," which perfectly describes the game's main action. The game gained popularity in the 20th century and has been studied extensively in mathematical game theory.
